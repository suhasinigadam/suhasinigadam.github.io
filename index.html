Let me introduce myself...


By hobby, I am a UI/UX Engineer. I love designing and creating simple, intuitive and fun user experiences that keep customers engaged and happy. I am passionate about design but I also love to code. The balance between the two helps me to understand the feasibility and application of each product. Each design sprint helps me learn something new and evolve as a UI/UX Engineer.

I am a Project Manager by profession. Project Management to me is the art of bringing people together to create a sustainable product in a fast-paced growth environment. I believe that it is not the whats and the hows that get things going but the whys that keep people motivated and focus-driven.

Web Design & Development:

User friendly and responsive designs that have a personality of their own. Focus on clean separate content holders for easy readability and top bar menus for quick navigation, along with attention to details.

Mobile App Design:

Efficient UI designs that help users navigate easily on small screens. Focus on target market and highlighting the features of the app in clear, easy user adaptable designs along with useful preview screens.

Print Design:

Good readability and clean design with a focus on getting the user to concentrate on the most important information, especially on small-sized paper. More attention given to color schemes, fonts and spacing.

User Experience:

Focus on user research, user flow charts, user analytics, simple functional prototypes and designs that not only facilitate easy user interaction but also provide delightful user experiences that would make customers want to come back.

Marketing:

Marketing for me has also encompassed pre-sales analysis, sales, branding, market testing and much more. Promotions and social media marketing through different strategies to break through to target markets.

Project Management:

Focus on all phases of project management, right from requirements engineering to the launch of the project. Planning and coordinating with people all around the globe and getting the work done on time and in budget.

Work Process

“If you can't describe what you are doing as a process,
you don't know what you're doing.”

It makes sense to me to break down things and simplify them, taking one step at a time, rather than breaking my head over something so complicated that I can't even wrap my head around it. I have a very simple work process that I follow all the time.

1. Idea
2. Sketch
3. Design
4. Develop
5. Test
6. Launch

Generate ideas, put them on paper, frame designs, develop the idea into reality, test for bugs and finally launch.
That's just it...Simple, isn't it?
